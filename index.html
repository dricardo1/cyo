Introduction

Inspired by those ol'-fashioned, choose-your-own adventure books, CYO (Choose Your Own) is a declarative framework for creating the same kind of experience on the web, with added features that you just couldn't get right in the old paperback books.

CYO is used to make choose your own adventure games. You don't need to know any JavaScript at all to use it.

The Choose Your Own Adventure Engine is built with Angular.js, and requires only HTML to use. A CYO story will run in any browser, and the styling can be fully customized with CSS.

Getting Started

CYO is a nearly all-directive library. To gain access to the directive, simply require the module as a dependency of your angular app.

Using the above script, any HTML bootstrapped to your "gameApp" module will have the following useful directives added.

Story Tag

To initialize a story, you use the storytag. The story tag has an attribute that points to the first chapter of your story, usually "intro".

Choice Tag

The story tag is pretty boring on its own. Things get a lot more interesting when you add a choice tag or two. Choices allow you to load a new page and keep the story going. When you click a choice, all the other choices on that page disappear. Let's update the intro to this story. 
    
    <page>
        <p>
          It was a very short story, and nothing at all happened.
        </p>
        <p>
          But, all of a sudden, you saw a strange man and he danced away down a grassy path.
        </p>
        <choice follow-him>
          Follow that dancing man!
        </choice>
        <choice stay-put>
          Stay put.
        </choice>
    </page>
You will now need a new file for each of those choices.

As you can see, the second option is disabled. That's because we haven't written that chapter. The buttons for unavailable chapters will be disabled.

That's about it for choice tags. Using just the choice tag, you can create a fun interactive story.

Event and Condition Tags

Things really get interesting with these two tags. Event and condition work together to show or hide parts of your story.

For example, in part of your story, the main character may happen to find a magic comb. Later, there might be a page that more than one page leads to, where the main character can use this comb to break out of a haunted mirror.

Event

Event has an attribute containing the name of an event, which is something that has happened in your story. 


<page>
    <p>
        You notice behind the bookshelf is an old candelabra.
        <i>This will come in handy, I'm sure.</i>
        <event found-candelabra></event>
    </p>
    <choice go-through-door>
        And now to continue exploring...
    </choice>
</page>

Now, for the remainder of your story, the found-candelabra event will be remembered. You can take advantage of that with the condition tag.

Condition

Conditions can optionally show part of your story based on if an event has happened previously. You can nest other events and other choices in side conditions, making them extremely powerful. 


<page>
    <p>
        The clown slowly approaches you, brandishing a rubber chicken menacingly.
    </p>
    <condition found-candelabra>
        <p>
           Suddenly, he recoils, shaking. His eyes fixed on the magic candelabra, he vanishes into a cloud of purple smoke.
        </p>
        <p>
            Hmm... that was weird.
        </p>
        <event slayed-clown></event>
        <choice keep-on-going>Well, gotta keep on going.</choice>
    </condition>
    <condition not found-candelabra>
        <p>
            Powerless to defend yourself, you are lightly chastised with rubber chicken until your friends come for you.
        </p>
        <p>
            THE END
        </p>
    </condition>
</page>

Notice we've used the not modifier to create an opportunity to include both possiblities. This tends to happen pretty commonly.

We've also included an event tag in the condition tag, allowing for powerful customization as stories grow in complexity.

Now let's put it all together. 


<!-- story/the-haunted-room.html -->
<page>
    <p>

    </p>

    </p>
    <choice behind-bookshelf>
        I should investigate this bookshelf.
    </choice>
    <choice go-through-door>
        I want to get out of here.
    </choice>
